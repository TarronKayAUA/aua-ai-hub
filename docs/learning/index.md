# Learning

Curated starting points for building artificial intelligence (AI) skills, organized by audience. Every link below was verified as live when this page was published. All resources are free unless noted.

## Faculty: getting started

For faculty who have not used AI tools seriously yet and want a structured, non-technical foundation.

- [Google AI Essentials](https://grow.google/ai-essentials/): a short self-paced course on using AI tools productively and responsibly at work. No technical background required.
- [Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/): a free university-built course from the University of Helsinki covering what AI is, what it can and cannot do, and how it affects society. Calibrated for complete beginners.
- [How LLMs Work](../basics/how-llms-work.md) on this site: a ten-minute plain-language primer to read before either course.

## Faculty: teaching with AI

For faculty integrating AI into courses, assessment, and curriculum, or setting expectations for students.

- [AAMC: Advancing AI Across Academic Medicine Resource Collection](https://www.aamc.org/about-us/mission-areas/medical-education/advancing-ai-across-academic-medicine-resource-collection): the Association of American Medical Colleges (AAMC) collection of practical resources for integrating AI into medical education.
- [AAMC: Principles for the Responsible Use of AI in and for Medical Education](https://www.aamc.org/about-us/mission-areas/medical-education/principles-ai-use): a concise statement of principles useful when drafting course policies.
- [DeepLearning.AI short courses](https://www.deeplearning.ai/short-courses/): one-to-two-hour hands-on courses on specific skills such as prompting and retrieval. Pick individual courses as needed rather than working through the catalog.
- [Anthropic Academy](https://www.anthropic.com/learn): free courses from Anthropic ranging from AI fluency for general audiences to technical material for builders.

## Students

For AUACOM students who want to use AI tools effectively and appropriately during medical school.

- [Elements of AI](https://www.elementsofai.com/): the best single free grounding in what these systems are. Pairs well with the [Common Misconceptions](../basics/misconceptions.md) page here.
- [Andrej Karpathy: Intro to Large Language Models](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zjkBMFhNj_g): a one-hour lecture explaining how large language models (LLMs) actually work, aimed at a general audience. The single best hour of video on the subject.
- The [tools directory](../tools/index.md) on this site: check a tool's governance status before using it for coursework, and never enter patient or student data into consumer AI tools.

## Going deeper (technical)

For anyone who wants to understand the machinery, not just use it.

- [3Blue1Brown: Neural networks series](https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/neural-networks/): visually outstanding explanations of neural networks, gradient descent, and transformers. The most accessible serious treatment available.
- [Andrej Karpathy: Intro to Large Language Models](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zjkBMFhNj_g): listed again here because it bridges the general and technical audiences well.
- [DeepLearning.AI short courses](https://www.deeplearning.ai/short-courses/): the more technical entries cover retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), agents, evals, and fine-tuning with working code.
- [Anthropic Academy](https://www.anthropic.com/learn): includes hands-on courses for building with the Claude application programming interface (API) and the Model Context Protocol (MCP).

## The medical education shelf

Current AI-in-medicine resources from the major professional bodies, worth checking a few times a year.

- [AAMC: Artificial Intelligence and Academic Medicine](https://www.aamc.org/about-us/mission-areas/medical-education/artificial-intelligence-and-academic-medicine): the AAMC's hub for AI initiatives, webinars, and guidance.
- [AMA: Augmented Intelligence in Medicine](https://www.ama-assn.org/practice-management/digital-health/augmented-intelligence-medicine): the American Medical Association (AMA) hub for AI policy, physician survey data, and practice guidance.
